Alliteration

Several words that begin with the same letter or sound.

The man on my corner pulls petals from rosebuds preparing them perfectly out on the street. He sells them from buckets. My eyes are enchanted. Pink puddles of petals float over his feet.

Betty Botter bought a bit of butter; “But,” she said, “this butter's bitter! If I put it in my batter It will make my batter bitter. But a bit o' better butter Will make my batter better.” Then she bought a bit o' butter Better than the bitter butter, Made her bitter batter better. So 'twas better Betty Botter Bought a bit o' better butter.

I'm standing in the shower and today I have to cope with a slimy slice of skinny-slippery flimsy dripping soap. I try to grip the sudsy strip to scrub my cruddy self and when I 'm clean I slide the silver on a corner shelf. Tomorrow when I shower, I close my eyes and hope I'll find it's been replaced by a fresher fatter soap.
